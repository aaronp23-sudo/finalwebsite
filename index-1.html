Jean-Michel Basquiat

Jean-Michel Basquiat (1960â€“1988) was a pioneering American artist whose work left an indelible mark on the contemporary art world. Born in Brooklyn to a Haitian father and Puerto Rican mother, Basquiat grew up immersed in multiple cultural influences, which would later shape the depth and diversity of his art. He first gained recognition in the late 1970s as a graffiti artist under the pseudonym SAMO, tagging cryptic phrases and poetic lines across Lower Manhattan. This early street art not only showcased his wit and social commentary but also laid the foundation for his entry into the gallery scene.
